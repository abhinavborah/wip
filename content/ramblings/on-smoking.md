## the article in question

![A realistic depiction of cavemen partying.](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:1400/format:webp/1*rtNbyR4ZA8Lkg1zX-hsA5w.jpeg)

> By [Abhinav Borah](https://medium.com/@heyabhinav?source=post_page---byline--eb1332e40340)

The discovery of fire has been crucial for man to pave his way into the modern world. When clashing flints ignited the wood, little did he anticipate the consequences that would soon follow. From providing vitality to life to ceasing it both through its flames, the fire has seen it all. One who is brave enough to tame fire can harness its true potential. And soon enough, the industrial revolution flourished and so did consumerism.

The idea of a burning cigarette is captivating. Being able to caress the flame while not letting it incinerate you can feel empowering. However, it is but a mere illusion as the fire eventually seeks its vengeance. The one who harnesses the flame finds his inevitable demise.

![The writer in question, smoking a cigarette.](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:1400/format:webp/1*26uRuLdy0QQulozznjuP7w.jpeg)

As a former smoker, this now brings me to ponder why people choose to smoke despite knowing the consequences. Some say experiences are the pillars of life and cigarettes are a medium to indulge in them; others substantiate their takes through nihilism. I find the latter quite intrusive. If life were to be indeed trivial and nothing matters in the grand scheme of things, should I relentlessly think about the impending doom while regurgitating how life has no intrinsic meaning? Should I discard reality and not be mindful of the beauty nature bestowed upon me? Contrary to the popular belief, Nietzsche never killed god nor did he disapprove of life in general. Pop-culture, a clear representation of _postmodernism gone rogue,_ has managed to misinterpret his works to fit their rhetoric and satiate teenage angst. Nietzsche wouldn’t have thought in his wildest dreams that [_his critique on The Age of Enlightenment_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/God_is_dead) would be transmogrified around just to fuel the horde of edgy teens and their bedroom posters.

![A Nietzsche greentext depicting the present-day scenario as we read.](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:1400/format:webp/1*p-OhX1OejY0AtmyRSIaUsg.jpeg)

Now considering my choice to quit smoking, I found myself in a downward spiral. Smoking started as a shabby cope with my stress and agony, but then turned into a leisurely act. I fear I haven’t been able to keep up with the promises I made to my younger self. I fear I have let him down. My younger self had high hopes and determination as he dreamed of being the harbinger of change. As he ventured along the path of life and arrived at adulthood, monotony and mediocrity prevailed. Being an adult, you are presented with the liberty of choice, which often goes unmediated. The worst case scenario, you tend to form a worldview which is secluded and devoid of criticism. I firmly believe experiences help a person grow holistically and the lack of the same hinders sensibility.

![The person reading this as they realize we’re all gonna make it.](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:1400/format:webp/1*FGxtTyXBREJiTYN_T9Wjnw.png)

On doing the much needed self-introspection, I realized I shouldn’t let the kid who once dreamed go oblivious; I decided to quit smoking for good. I no longer seek to be a contrarian and be shrouded in a veil of disbelief. One might emulate the Socratic method to arrive at a conclusion whether smoking is justified or should be eradicated at all costs. I understand an individual governs their life and has the absolute right to live according to their wishes, but I also believe keeping a check upon your well-being and your well-wishers is the least one can do. Though I’m still naive and inexperienced when it comes to life, I would prefer to live long enough to see my grandchildren grow.
